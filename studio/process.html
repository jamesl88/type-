process

+ Initial consultation

A meeting is arranged to discuss your brief and requirements whereby a scope of works is formed listing out the services we that we will provide and the likely costs involved. The developed brief will be confirmed in writing before any work begins.

+ Survey

The property may require a measured survey depending on the project type before we can begin to start the appraisals. We will note any site and planning constraints, possible

+ Feasibility studies

We assist the client by appraising the site, and this involves the design brief in the first stage of the process (RIBA stages A and B). Design options are presented where we provide the strengths and weaknesses of each option, giving the preferred option and also allow for integration with the client’s brief through consultation.

+ Design workshop

Each client gets the opportunity to engage with the initial proposals; we focus on the client’s needs and we achieve this by communicating the ideas effectively so the initial proposals presented receive a responsive outcome. Design decisions are refined during the workshop and the proposals are agreed then developed up to planning stage.

+ Planning consent

Once the proposal is established and agreed, we seek the planning authority for pre-application advice at an early stage to identify any key issues to ensure that these are addressed before submitting the application. We ensure that we communicate with the client throughout this process.

+ Building regulations

Information will require building regulations compliance, and at this stage a structural engineer may need to be approached for structural calculations. A package of building regulation drawings are provided for the building inspector for approval.

+ Detail drawings

Depending on the scale and complexity of the project, the appropriate drawing package can be agreed at the outset. Construction, materials and schedules are provided for construction stage for the contractor to have the information to start work on site.

+ On-site

We can offer a service to provide site observations throughout the build, and can administer the building contract on the client’s behalf. This scope of work can be agreed before the start of the project.
